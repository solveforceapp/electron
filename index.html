Electron

Etymology: From Greek ēlektron (ἤλεκτρον), meaning “amber”—the ancient source of static electricity and the root of the word electricity.
“The electron is the fundamental carrier of electricity, the invisible thread weaving together the fabric of modern technology.”

The electron is a subatomic particle with a negative charge, discovered in 1897 by J.J. Thomson. Its movement underpins all electrical phenomena and is the foundation of electronics, computation, and communication.

Electronics: From Amber to AI

The journey from rubbing amber in ancient Greece to today’s microprocessors is the story of the electron. Electronics harness the flow of electrons to create circuits, devices, and networks—powering everything from smartphones to satellites.
